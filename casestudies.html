Project Overview

Duration: 4 months (September 2022 - December 2022)
Efflorescence is a flower catalogue app designed to help users decide what flowers and bouquets would best suit their wants and needs. Our flower catalogue will let users discover and purchase flowers for pickup which will affect customers who need flowers for certain occasions by organizing and recommending flowers by occasions. We will measure effectiveness by tracking indecisive customers who need flowers.

User Journey Maps

James Marley is a busy working man who wants to purchase beautiful flowers so that he can make a good first impression for his upcoming date.
This is his course of action:

Step 1: Go to the flower shop.
  • Locate flower shop
  • Physically head to the flower shop

Step 2: Browse the flower shop.
  • Look at different kinds of flowers in the shop

Step 3: Ask the florist for recommendations.
  • Ask florist the most popular flowers
  • Ask about flowers for dates

Step 4: Purchase flowers.
  • Browse the recommended flowers
  • Select flower based on aesthetics

Step 5: Have date.
  • Go to the date spot
  • Give the date the flowers
  • Enjoy the rest of the date

Rose Owens is part of the lower working class who wants to purchase an affordable bouquet so that she can place them for her ex-husband's burial.
This is her course of action:

Step 1: Go to the flower shop.
  • Locate the cheapest flower shop
  • Locate nearest flower shop

Step 2: Ask for recommendations.
  • Ask the florist which flowers are the cheapest

Step 3: Browse through recommendations.
  • Decide which flowers are funeral-approved
  • Pick the cheapest flowers

Step 4: Purchase flowers.
  • Pay for flowers
  • Wrap flowers up

Step 5: Attend burial.
  • Take a car to the burial site
  • Set flowers down
  • Mourn for loss

An app could solve both James' and Rose's problem.


For James Marley, the flower catalogue app can allow him to search for bouquets and flowers that may best suit his date. He will also be able to purchase the flowers in advance so that he can pick it up without rushing.


For Rose Owens, having a catalogue may not necessarily help her but being able to sort the bouquets and flowers like in James Marley's case may help. She will be able to search for the low-cost options as well as include a discount code if needed.

Wireframes

From paper wireframes to digital wireframes. I first designed 5 pages for the app.


Home Page:

I decided to create a navigation bar on the bottom to accommodate iPhone users since they no longer have built-in navigation buttons. The welcoming introduction allows users to identify if they are logged in or on the correct account. The featured slideshow allows the users to view popular and trending flowers/ bouquets.

Discover Page:

I left this page with only images of flowers and bouquets so users could have an idea or spark some inspiration. I also added a sort button to allow the users to specify what they are looking for.

Browse Page:

I decided to add the quiz banner here for in case they wanted to have a fun time taking one and may introduce them to flowers that they may be interested in. At the top, users will also be able to search for a specific item if they know what they would like.

Low-Fidelity Prototype

Usability Study For Low-Fi Prototype

Research Study Plan

Research Questions:

  • Which features (page) of this app did users find most useful?
  • Where in this app do users get confused (if they do)?
  • Did users find what they wanted to do in this app and if so, what was it?
  • What features would users like to see more of?
  • Did users need help during the app and when did they need help?

KPIs:

  • Completion of System Usability Scale (SUS)
  • Conversion Rates- time it takes to get from the Cart page to the Checkout Page
  • Use of navigation VS search

Methodology:

  • Moderated Usability Study
  • Location: Remote through Discord
  • Dates: October 13, 2022 - October 14, 2022
  • 5 participants
  • Each session will last 30 minutes and will need to browse and purchase an item.

Participants:

  • People who purchase flowers online and in-person
  • People who purchase flowers more than once a year
  • Both men and women who are between the ages 16-50

Research Results

From the research that I have conducted for the Low-Fidelity Prototype:

  1. Users could not purchase more than one item.
  2. Users do not understand the purpose of the quiz.
  3. Some users choose options on the homescreen.
  4. Some users were confused because there were no images.

I have prioritized the insights as follows:
Priority 0:
  1. Users need to be able to go to the cart on the home screen.
  2. Users need better jargon so they understand the app's functionality.
Priority 1:
  1. Improve the navigation bar to include appropriate sections.
Priority 2:
  1. Add relevant images to the app.

Conclusion

Takeaways:

Users have said that the app is clear and easy to navigate. It also has a simple and clean design to it. I learned that it's best to keep the colors minimal to avoid distractions. I also learned that there should be multiple methods in navigating to different screens. This will help with the user's experience.

Next Steps:

  • I would like to change the visual design of this app to make it more appealing.
  • I would also like to create more animations to make the app more accessible and pleasing for the user's experience.
  • I would conduct more user research and focus more on solving the persona's problems.

Project Overview

Efflorescence is a flower catalogue app designed to help users decide what flowers and bouquets would best suit their wants and needs.

User Research

User Journey Maps

James Marley is a busy working man who wants to purchase beautiful flowers so that he can make a good first impression for his upcoming date.
This is his course of action:

Step 1: Go to the flower shop.

Step 2: Browse the flower shop.

Step 3: Ask the florist for recommendations.

Step 4: Purchase flowers.

Step 5: Have date.

Rose Owens is part of the lower working class who wants to purchase an affordable bouquet so that she can place them for her ex-husband's burial.
This is her course of action:

Step 1: Go to the flower shop.

Step 2: Ask florist for recommendations.

Step 3: Browse through recommendations.

Step 4: Purchase flowers.

Step 5: Attend burial.

An app could solve both James' and Rose's problem.
For James Marley, the flower catalogue app can allow him to search for bouquets and flowers that may best suit his date. He will also be able to purchase the flowers in advance so that he can pick it up without rushing.
For Rose Owens, having a catalogue may not necessarily help her but being able to sort the bouquets and flowers like in James Marley's case may help. She will be able to search for the low-cost options as well as include a discount code if needed.

Usability Studies

Research Study Plan

From the research that I have conducted:
  1. Users could not purchase more than one item.
  2. Users do not understand the purpose of the quiz.
  3. Some users choose options on the homescreen.
  4. Some users were confused because there were no images.

I have prioritized the insights as follows:
Priority 0:
  1. Users need to be able to go to the cart on the home screen.
  2. Users need better jargon so they understand the app's functionality.
Priority 1:
  1. Improve the navigation bar to include appropriate sections.
Priority 2:
  1. Add relevant images to the app.
